## 5.11 Detection Information Is Real, Regime-Conditional, and Cross-Period Stable, Yet Sub-Cost (C6)

Sections 5.9–5.10 established that the *identified* LLM contribution to A-share onset detection is ≈0. A natural rescue hypothesis remains: perhaps the onset signal lives not in any single feature marginally, but **conditionally** — a candlestick "onset" shape may be informative only once the market regime is known, exactly as a genomic promoter is transcribed only in the presence of the right transcription factor. This subsection tests that hypothesis to its conclusion with three nested measurements, and reports a precise negative result: the conditional information is real, cross-period stable, and even directionally monotone, but its magnitude falls **below the transaction-cost floor**, so it does not constitute tradable alpha. We present this as measurement; the *sub-cost localization* is the point.

All quantities use the geometry/relative-position candlestick feature set of Section 4.3 on the D1 panel (Section 5.1), the 5-day forward return $r_5$ (and its per-date cross-sectional residual), and point-in-time regime states $Z$ (trailing-return-sign *trend*, trailing-volatility tercile, and the disaster-month filter of Section 4.1). Code, committed JSON outputs, and hermetic unit tests are listed under *Reproducibility* below.

**Measurement 1 — Does the regime add information? (interaction information with a label-permutation null.)** For feature $X$, target $Y=r_5$, and regime $Z$, the quantity of interest is the *interaction information* $\mathrm{II}=I(X;Y\mid Z)-I(X;Y)$ [mcgill1954; coverthomas2006]: information that exists only conditional on the regime. A subtlety governs the null. The naive test — permuting $Y$ *within* $Z$-strata — only asks whether $I(X;Y\mid Z)>0$, which is *trivially* true whenever the marginal $I(X;Y)>0$, and at $n=2\times10^5$ the permutation $p$-value floors at $1/(\text{n\_perm}+1)$ for any non-zero estimate (binned MI is positively biased; significance must come from a permutation null, not the raw estimate). The correct null for "the regime *adds* information" instead permutes the **$Z$-labels** (preserving the $(X,Y)$ pairing and hence the marginal relation, destroying only the three-way structure), so a positive result certifies that the *real* regime buys more than a random regime of identical granularity [ojala2010permutation]. On quantile-binned estimates ($n=200{,}000$ rows, 8 bins, 300 permutations), **52 of 78** (feature $\times$ target $\times$ regime) combinations show $\mathrm{II}>0$ at the permutation floor. The *trend* regime dominates: for the leading features the interaction is $\approx70$–$76\%$ of the conditional MI — i.e. roughly three-quarters of these features' (small) information about $r_5$ exists only once the trend regime is known.

**Measurement 2 — Is the conditional information cross-period stable?** Pooled dependence at large $n$ is necessary, not sufficient: the same non-stationarity that voids return edges (Section 5.6) can void information. Recomputing the interaction test per calendar year for the six leading hits (capped at 150K rows/year), **all 6 are positive and permutation-significant in every year 2022–2025** (e.g. `close_pct_prior` $\times$ *trend*: $\mathrm{II}=+0.0060, +0.0023, +0.0103, +0.0054$ nats for 2022–2025). The conditional-information hypothesis survives the cross-period test that the return edge does not (Figure `motif_mi_summary`). We stress the honest caveat: the per-year $p$-values are floored by sample size and certify only $\mathrm{II}>0$, not a *large* effect — the absolute conditional MI is $\approx 0.006$ nats (a correlation-equivalent of $\approx 0.07$–$0.14$).

**Measurement 3 — Is the information directional, and does it survive cost? (mutual information is sign-blind.)** Mutual information cannot distinguish $E[Y\mid X,Z]$ moving with $X$ (directional, long-tradable) from $\mathrm{Var}[Y\mid X,Z]$ moving with $X$ (volatility/risk, not directly tradable). We therefore measure the conditional *mean* directly: a per-regime rank-slope of $X$ vs $Y$, a monotonicity coefficient (rank correlation of feature-quantile-bucket index vs bucket-mean return), and a directional fraction (share of bucket dispersion in the mean vs the variance). The leading features have a **perfectly monotone** conditional-mean response within the trend regime (monotonicity coefficient $\approx 1.0$, rank-slope $0.083$–$0.102$), so the information is *not* pure sign-blind risk — but only $\approx 37$–$43\%$ of their predictive dispersion is in the mean; the majority is sign-blind variance. The decisive test is then the *simplest possible* model that the information would support: in the activating (trend-up) regime, rank cross-sectionally by a single feature and hold the long-only top decile, cash otherwise, on non-overlapping 5-day periods, net of a realistic A-share round-trip cost of $\approx 0.2\%$ (asymmetric stamp duty + commission + slippage; Section 5.6), with date-clustered bootstrap CIs and per-year splits. Across the three leading features the regime gate **improves** the net Sharpe over an always-on variant in every case ($-1.18$ vs $-1.89$; $-2.35$ vs $-2.71$; $-1.39$ vs $-2.04$) — confirming the regime contributes real, correctly-signed value — yet the gated net Sharpe is **negative every year (0/4 positive)** with mean-excess CIs lying strictly below zero (e.g. `close_pct_prior` mean-excess $95\%$ CI $[-0.0043,\,-0.0003]$ per period). The gross directional edge ($\approx 0.1\%$ per 5 days) sits *below* the cost floor (Figure `motif_tradability`).

**Verdict and contribution.** Onset detection information in public candlestick features is real, concentrated in a context-conditional ("onset-motif") structure, cross-period stable, and even directionally monotone — yet its magnitude is **sub-cost**, so it is information, not tradable alpha. This sharpens the paper's thesis (*detection $\neq$ edge*) from a backtest observation into an information-theoretic localization: the binding constraint is neither absence of signal, nor sign-blindness, nor non-stationarity, but transaction cost — consistent with the broader anomaly-erosion literature [novymarx2016taxonomy] and with regime-conditional return structure being long known [cooper2004market]. As with C3 and C5, the *components* are standard — interaction information dates to [mcgill1954], conditional MI to [coverthomas2006], permutation inference to [ojala2010permutation] — and we claim neither. Our contribution is (i) a reusable **three-layer falsification protocol** (label-permutation interaction test $\rightarrow$ directional/sign-blind decomposition $\rightarrow$ net-of-cost regime-gated backtest) that decides, before any expensive modeling, whether a candidate pattern is worth a model; and (ii) the precise finding that the A-share onset motif fails at the *cost* layer specifically, having passed the information and direction layers — a result that closes the "maybe it's conditional" rescue hypothesis cleanly rather than leaving it open.

**Reproducibility.** The protocol is CPU-only and runs on the committed D1 panel at zero LLM cost. Estimators and their hermetic tests: `src/onset/mutual_info.py` (marginal/conditional MI, within-stratum and label-permutation nulls; `tests/algo/test_mutual_info.py`), `src/onset/regimes.py` (point-in-time regime states; `test_regimes.py`), `src/onset/tradability.py` (directionality/monotonicity/variance-vs-mean; `test_tradability.py`), `src/onset/regime_gate.py` (regime-gated long-only excess; `test_regime_gate.py`). Real runs (each writes a JSON under `results/motif/`): `python -m src.onset.run_mi_probe` (Measurement 1 → `mi_probe.json`), `run_mi_stability` (Measurement 2 → `mi_stability.json`), `run_tradability` (Measurement 3 direction → `tradability.json`), `run_regime_gate_bt` (Measurement 3 cost → `regime_gate_bt.json`), and `summarize_motif` / `summarize_tradability` for the verdicts and figures. Each synthetic test encodes a *known* structure (e.g. an XOR target informative only given $Z$; a sign-blind target with $\mathrm{Var}[Y\mid X]\!\propto\!|X|$ but $E[Y\mid X]=0$) so the estimators are validated to detect conditional information and to *reject* sign-blind dependence as non-tradable.
